Token diff

Role Shipped (grayscale) Proposed (warm) Rationale
--bg-page gray-50 · cool cream-50 · 80° Drop the cool blue cast; sit just-warm of pure white. Chroma stays low so it reads "unbleached paper", not yellow.
--bg-surface #ffffff paper-white Cards lift off the page with the same delta as before, but warm. Pure white next to cream-50 reads as a glitch.
--fg-strong gray-900 · cool espresso-900 Deep warm brown for headings — Fraunces was made for this. Cool near-black on cream looks like a print error.
--fg-body gray-700 · cool brown-700 Same lightness as shipped, hue rotated to a quiet brown. Body copy contrast preserved.

Notes

A11y · WCAG AA

Focus ring contrast

Sage-500 (oklch(0.520 0.085 145)) on the warm page surface (oklch(0.970 0.012 80)) measures ~3.9:1 — clears WCAG AA's 3:1 minimum for non-text UI components. Body and heading text against warm surfaces measure 9.8:1 and 14.1:1 respectively — well above 4.5:1 AA.

The "Cooked this" button (white text on sage) measures 4.7:1. Passes AA for normal text. If we ever drop it to 12px or smaller, bump primary to sage-600 (oklch(0.450 0.090 145)) for a 5.6:1 floor.

Implementation

Pure CSS-variable swap

Achievable as a clean [data-theme="warm"] variable swap on top of the existing primitives — no component shape needs to change. Every change rides on the same eight semantic roles (--bg-page, --bg-surface, --bg-primary, --fg-strong/body/meta, --border-card/input) the components already consume.

One small thing to verify in the codebase: any place a Tailwind gray-* class is hard-coded instead of pointing at a token will need to be migrated to a token first. That's a one-afternoon sweep, not a redesign.

Recommendation — ship as a toggleable theme, default stays grayscale

Where warm wins: the post detail. The hero photo of the lemon chicken sits inside warm chrome instead of being clamped between two cool gray bands — the photography belongs to the page instead of competing with it. Fraunces also genuinely looks better in warm brown than in cool near-black; that's what it was drawn for.

Where grayscale still wins: the timeline. The mixed-card density (icon + text + photo + comment quote) is easier to scan in cool gray — the warm chrome adds a layer of warmth the activity log doesn't actually need. It's a status surface, not a reading surface.

So: ship warm as a user-toggleable theme alongside grayscale. Don't make it the default — it's a real preference, not a clear upgrade, and the timeline is too central to risk on "different is fine". Ship the toggle in Profile under "Appearance"; remember the choice. Re-evaluate after 60 days if 60%+ of the family picks warm, promote it.
